Flight canceled or delayed? Here’s what to do next

Airline delays and cancellations are happening frequently. Here's how to avoid getting stuck if they happen to you.

Flight delays and cancellations affected a significant portion of U.S. travelers in 2025, with over 23% experiencing delays and nearly 1.5% facing cancellations. Travelers can minimize disruptions by monitoring flight status through airline apps and third-party tools, signing up for notifications and understanding incoming aircraft status. Knowing passenger rights is crucial, including eligibility for refunds and reimbursement for certain expenses during significant delays or cancellations, especially for controllable disruptions. Acting quickly, considering standby options, utilizing lounge access and leveraging travel protections from select payment methods can help manage unexpected travel interruptions. Refunds are available for significant delays or cancellations if you decline alternative transportation, with timelines set for reimbursement based on payment method. Weather-related disruptions are typically excluded from many airline guarantees, but travel advisories may allow for flexible rebooking without additional charges. Some travel protections, such as trip delay coverage or reimbursement for extra expenses, may only apply if you used an eligible payment method to book your flight.

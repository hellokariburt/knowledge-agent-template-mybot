Everything you need to know if you have a trip to Mexico coming up

Here's everything you should know if you have a trip to Mexico coming up, considering the cartel violence that happened this week.

Recent civil unrest in Mexico, particularly in Jalisco, led to temporary travel disruptions and a shelter-in-place order, which has now been lifted. Airports and hotels in affected areas like Guadalajara and Puerto Vallarta have resumed normal operations, though intermittent delays may persist. Travelers are advised to reconsider trips to Jalisco, Michoacan and Nayarit until stability is confirmed, and to book flexible travel arrangements. Other popular destinations, such as Cancun, Mexico City and Oaxaca, remain largely unaffected and continue to operate normally. Travel waivers are available for certain flights to affected airports, but eligibility and terms depend on the airline and travel dates. Most travel insurance policies do not cover cancellations due to civil unrest unless purchased before the event became known; 'cancel for any reason' coverage offers broader protection. Cruise itineraries to Puerto Vallarta may be rerouted, while Caribbean ports remain unaffected.

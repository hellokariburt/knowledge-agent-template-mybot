It's worth double-checking: My credit card's rental car coverage paid out for damage I thought was excluded

After shredding a tire on a Fiji pothole, I assumed my credit card wouldn't cover it. Filing the claim anyway taught me a valuable lesson.

A travel writer recounts a rental car mishap in Fiji that led to unexpected lessons about credit card rental car coverage. Despite initial doubts, a claim for a damaged tire was ultimately reimbursed after clarifying the policy details with the benefits administrator. Primary rental car insurance offered by some premium credit cards can cover more scenarios than many travelers realize, especially for sudden damage from road hazards. The article emphasizes the importance of reviewing official policy documents and contacting the benefits provider for clarification before assuming a claim will be denied. Coverage terms often exclude wear and tear, gradual deterioration or mechanical breakdown, but sudden damage from an external event may be eligible. Eligibility for primary rental car insurance typically requires paying for the entire rental with the card and declining the rental agency’s collision coverage. Coverage limits, eligible rental durations and vehicle types vary by card, so reviewing the specific policy is essential.

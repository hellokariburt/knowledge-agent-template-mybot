First look: Inside Korean Air’s stunning new flagship lounge at LAX

Korean Air unveils its newly renovated two-level flagship lounge at LAX, featuring elevated dining, luxury design and exclusive SkyTeam access.

Korean Air has unveiled a newly renovated two-level lounge at Los Angeles International Airport's Tom Bradley International Terminal, opening March 6. The space features modern Korean design, curated artwork and expanded amenities including a full on-site kitchen, upgraded shower suites and dedicated quiet areas. The lounge is divided into Prestige Class, Miler Club and an exclusive First Class area, each offering tailored seating, dining and service experiences. Access is available to eligible Korean Air and SkyTeam premium passengers and elite members, but not to premium economy ticket holders without elite status. The lounge is most convenient for departures from the Tom Bradley International Terminal; SkyTeam passengers departing from other terminals may need alternate options. Menus feature both Korean comfort dishes and Western selections, with a la carte dining for First Class and live stations for Prestige and Miler guests. Shower amenities differ by lounge section, with premium brands provided for First Class and other high-tier travelers.

What to do if your flight from — or through — the Middle East is affected this week

If you're traveling from or through a Middle Eastern airport this week, you're most likely dealing with travel disruptions and cancellations. Here's what you need to know.

Travel disruptions and cancellations are widespread across the Middle East due to recent military conflict, with over 9,500 flights affected and major airports like Dubai and Doha experiencing closures or limited operations. Travelers stranded in the region are advised to follow official directives, register for government alert programs, and keep travel documents ready for possible departures. Some governments, such as the United Arab Emirates, are providing assistance like hotel and meal coverage for stranded travelers. Flight waivers and refunds may be available, but travelers should check with their airline and be proactive in rebooking or canceling flights. Travel insurance typically excludes coverage for war or civil unrest, and new bookings are not covered for this event as it is now a known issue. Rebooking with a different carrier may not be reimbursed unless specifically allowed by your original airline's waiver. Written documentation with airlines and insurance providers is recommended for any claims or disputes.

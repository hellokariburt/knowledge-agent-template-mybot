How to travel solo — and why you absolutely should

If you want to travel but the only thing holding you back is someone to do it with, you should seriously consider going alone.

Solo travel offers unmatched freedom to set your own schedule and pursue personal interests without compromise. Traveling alone can make it easier to find deals, especially for flights using points or miles, and often leads to meeting new people. Popular solo travel destinations include easy-to-navigate cities, relaxing beach spots and places with vibrant hostel scenes. Certain destinations, such as romantic resorts or family-focused attractions, may be less enjoyable for solo travelers. Solo trips can sometimes feel lonely, particularly during meals or extended downtime. Decision-making and problem-solving fall entirely on you, which may be challenging for those who prefer shared responsibilities. Safety and comfort can be enhanced by sharing your itinerary, packing light and choosing social accommodations like hostels.
